My Research Interest

HCI In Education

I'm incredibly passionate about integrating technology into education, specifically through XR and AI. This research topic excites me because it's at the intersection of my academic background in HCI and my personal goal to make education more accessible and engaging. What's thrilling is the potential of XR to transform learning experiences, especially for marginalized groups. This isn't just about technology; it's about creating a fair and inclusive educational environment for everyone, regardless of their background or abilities. I've seen firsthand how technology can bridge gaps and I'm driven to explore this further.

HCI - Accessibility Research

Conducted research on ASL facial expressions using "mediapipe". Investigated privacy techniques, such as dynamic video filters, to mask non-essential facial details during sign language data collection. This data can be used to enhance AI models for sign language recognition and translation.

Advancements in VR/AR in Science Education

  • From the perspective of human-computer interaction methods and learner-centered views, it includes collaborative learning among students in a virtual teaching environment.

  • This paper mainly cites new papers within the last five years, incorporating updated technologies and application scenarios, such as the application of XR technology in STEM education during the pandemic.

  • The paper also includes a sociological perspective, summarizing issues of unequal distribution of educational resources, as well as accessibility and inclusivity issues such as marginalized groups, people with disabilities (ASL), and mental illness sufferers in the application of XR technology.

  • The application scenarios cited in this paper are XR devices that can be used independently (Meta Quest 3 and HoloLens), excluding devices that rely on PC graphics cards and external motion sensors, as well as XR devices for mobile phones and tablets.
